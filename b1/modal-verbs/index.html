Modal Verbs

Explanation

Modal verbs are used to express ability, possibility, necessity, advice, or permission.

  • Can – ability: I can swim.
  • Must – necessity: You must wear a seatbelt.
  • Should – advice: You should study harder.
  • May/Might – possibility: It might rain tomorrow.

Modals come before infinitive verbs and the “to” is removed. Also, modal verbs do not need to match their subject in plural agreement

The nine most common modals are can, could, shall, should, will, would, may, might, and must.

Sentence Structure

Affirmative -> Subject + modal verb + verb (without to)

  • She can play the guitar.
  • You may sit here.
  • It might snow tomorrow.

Negative -> Subject + modal verb + not + verb

  • I don’t have to finish my homework tonight.
  • She wouldn’t help even if she had time.
  • Bob shouldn’t eat so much fast food.

Questions -> Subject + modal verb + not + verb

  • Must we wear helmets?
  • Might she be at the library now?
  • Will Mary be at the meeting tomorrow?

Exercises

1. Write 3 sentences with can about your abilities.
2. Transform: "I am obliged to go to school" → with must.
3. Make 2 sentences with should (advice).
4. Write 2 possibilities for tomorrow using may or might.
